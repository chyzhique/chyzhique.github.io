CONDITIONAL SENTENCES

FOCUS ON USE

A. Look at the examples below taken from the texts and answer the questions that follow.

1. If the technology was available right now, patients would even be spared of a trip to the doctor's.
2. ...if new discoveries are made, the program will simply be updated to include them ...
3. ...if patients are distracted, anxiety is reduced and the procedure becomes less painful.
• Do the sentences refer to the past or present/future?
• Which sentence expresses something which generally happens?
• Which sentence expresses something which is possible to happen in the future?
• Which sentence expresses something imaginary?
• In which of the sentences could if be replaced by when?

FOCUS ON FORM

Look at the sentences 1-3 again and complete the rules below.

0. Zero Conditional = a general truth or something that always happens

if or when + Present Simple →

1. Conditional Sentences Type 1 = something which is possible to happen in the present or future

if / when + → will, can, must, may, might or should + base form
→ imperative

2. Conditional Sentences Type 2 = something imaginary/unreal or unlikely to happen in the present or future

if + → would, could + base form
• Unless can be used instead of if... not... in conditional sentences.
• If I were you is often used to express an opinion or give advice.
