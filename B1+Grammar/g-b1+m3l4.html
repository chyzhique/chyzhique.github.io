SHOULD / OUGHT TO / HAD BETTER

A. Read the examples below and answer the questions that follow.

1.
You ought to call the police if he threatens you again.
You should see a doctor if the pain continues.
• Is there any difference in meaning between the two verbs in bold?
• What is the speaker doing in each sentence?
2.
I think he'd better stay in bed for another day.
Alex should be home by 6:00 o'clock.
• In which sentence does the verb mean is expected to?
• What does the verb in the other sentence mean?
3.
I should have handed in my research paper yesterday.
• Did the speaker hand in his research paper yesterday?
• How does the speaker feel about it?
4.
'Mum, my stomach hurts!'
'You know, Rod, you shouldn't have eaten all that chocolate.'
• Did Rod eat a lot of chocolate?
• Does his mother think he did the right thing?

Complete the sentences with should or shouldn't and the correct form of the verbs in brackets.

  1. You (try) to get at least 6-8 hours of sleep each night.

  2. Karen didn't do well in her exams. She (study) harder for them.

  3. Jose (lie) to his best friend, George. Now George can't trust him anymore.

  4. You (take) a nice long holiday. I'm sure it will relax you.

  5. Michael (play) football until a doctor examines his knee.
