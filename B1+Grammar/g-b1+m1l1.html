PRESENT SIMPLE / PRESENT PROGRESSIVE

FOCUS ON USE

A. Match the rules about the uses of the Present Simple with the examples below.

1. permanent situations a. Ray, a professor, lives in Germany.
2. repeated habitual actions b. The computer and the phone make it easy to avoid talking to each other face-to-face.
3. general truths c. We talk online every day, sometimes for hours.
4. future actions related to timetables and programmes. d. Tania's flight arrives at 9 am tomorrow morning.

B. Match the rules about the uses of the Present Progressive with the examples below.

The Present Progressive is used for:

5. actions happening now a. Ray is looking for a job in Boston ...
6. temporary situations b. She's actually coming to France to see me this summer, ...
7. future arrangements c. Listen! Our neighbors are arguing again.
8. situations which are changing or developing around the present time d. You are always complaining about everything!
9. annoying actions which happen repeatedly (with always, continually, etc.) e. Communication is becoming easier thanks to technological developments.
