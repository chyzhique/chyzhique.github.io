REPORTED SPEECH (statements)

Read the examples, answer the questions and complete the rules

DIRECT SPEECHREPORTED SPEECH
• 'You should play Jay a few songs from my new album,' Sean said to Vicky. • Sean told Vicky that she should play Jay a few songs from his new album.
• 'You are the best I have heard for ages,' the studio engineer said to them. • The studio engineer told them that they were the best he had heard for ages.
• 'I want to listen to Sean's CD,' Jay said. • Jay said that he wanted to listen to Sean's CD.
  1. When do we use reported speech?
  2. In the examples above, which words have changed in reported speech and how?

The Rules:

• Say and tell are reporting verbs. Use before an indirect object (person) and when there is no indirect object.

• Pronouns and possessive adjectives change according to the meaning of the sentence.
