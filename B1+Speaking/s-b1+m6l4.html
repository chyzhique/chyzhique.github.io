"earthquake;"

An earthquake is a sudden shaking or trembling of the Earth's surface caused by the movement of tectonic plates beneath the Earth's crust.

Synonyms: tremor, quake, seismic activity

"flood;"

A flood is an overflow of water onto normally dry land, often caused by heavy rainfall, melting snow, or dam failures.

Synonyms: deluge, inundation, overflow

"tornado;"

A tornado is a violent and destructive rotating column of air characterized by a funnel shape, often accompanied by high winds and severe weather conditions.

Synonyms: twister, cyclone, whirlwind

"tsunami;"

A tsunami is a series of large ocean waves, typically caused by underwater earthquakes, volcanic eruptions, or other disturbances, that can result in widespread coastal destruction.

Synonyms: tidal wave, seismic sea wave, ocean surge

"forest fire;"

A forest fire is a wildfire that occurs in forested areas and can spread rapidly, often causing extensive damage to trees, vegetation, and wildlife.

Synonyms: wildfire, bushfire, woodland fire

"volcanic eruption;"

A volcanic eruption is the release of magma, ash, gases, and other materials from a volcano, often resulting in explosions and the ejection of volcanic debris.

Synonyms: volcano eruption, volcanic explosion, volcanic outburst

"financial;"

Financial relates to matters concerning money, income, expenses, investments, and financial resources.

Synonyms: economic, monetary, fiscal

"medical;"

Medical pertains to the field of medicine, healthcare, and the diagnosis, treatment, and prevention of diseases and medical conditions.

Synonyms: healthcare, medicinal, clinical

"psychological (help);"

Psychological help refers to assistance and support provided by mental health professionals or counselors to address emotional and psychological issues.

Synonyms: emotional support, counseling, therapy

"live in safe homes;"

Living in safe homes means residing in dwellings that are secure, free from hazards, and designed to protect occupants from various risks.

Synonyms: reside in secure homes, live in safe residences

"be well-informed;"

Being well-informed means having knowledge and information about current events, developments, or important matters.

Synonyms: stay informed, keep up-to-date, stay knowledgeable

"not panic;"

Not panicking means remaining calm and composed, especially in stressful or emergency situations, to make rational decisions.

Synonyms: stay composed, remain calm, keep cool

"follow instructions from authorities;"

Following instructions from authorities means obeying the guidance and directives provided by government or emergency personnel during crises or disasters.

Synonyms: heed guidance from authorities, comply with directives
