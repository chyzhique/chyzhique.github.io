A1. Elementary

⚠️ DIFFICULTY: 🔴
Get to know:
📌 basic phrases
📌 self-introductions
📌 simple interactions
📌 how to ask and answer questions about personal details
  1. You can easily make simple sentences and simple daily conversations.
  2. A2 English level is the minimum recruitment level for some companies.
  3. It is not a sufficient level for academic education.
  4. You have command of the language on specific subjects, routine and simple tasks.
  5. You can understand and convey messages in English, respond to simple and direct information, have simple face-to-face conversations, and talk about activities you like or dislike.
  6. You can learn and meet daily and simple needs using your knowledge of English; shopping, vacation planning or hotel reservation etc.
BACK

A2. Pre-intermediate

⚠️ DIFFICULTY: 🔴🔴 Get to know: 📌 Popular expressions related to everyday life 📌 Simple communications 📌 Exchange of information on familiar and routine matters. 📌 How to describe ordinary ideas
  1. You can do most uncomplicated tasks.
  2. You master routine social and work situations.
  3. Even if you make some mistakes, you can start and maintain a conversation in English.
  4. You begin to build your subject-specific English vocabulary;
  5. You can give professional instructions, explain a simple problem and come up with a solution, guide your visitors, explain in detail and politely respond to invitations.
  6. You can state your preferences, agreements or disagreements in English or make a complaint.
  7. You can grasp knowledge of unfamiliar topics and have long English conversations with others on a wide variety of topics.
  8. However, you may not yet be able to maintain consistency in long English phrases or unfamiliar topics.
  9. You can describe equipment you know, arrange and negotiate an agreement, establish professional contacts and advice in English.
  10. While speaking English, you may feel that your native language is interfering at times.
BACK

B1. Intermediate

⚠️ DIFFICULTY: 🔴🔴🔴 Get to know: 📌 how to understand the main ideas 📌 how to deal with most situations 📌 how to write decent text & letters 📌 how to describe experiences, give reasons and explanations for opinions and plans.
  1. B1 English level is the minimum level of recruitment that many companies will work with a foreign team in departments such as project management, foreign sales and exports.
  2. At B1 level, you can initiate, maintain and end most communicative tasks required for your personal and business needs.
  3. You can understand the instructions and give direct instructions.
  4. You can easily communicate in English in social and professional situations – including participating in discussions and meetings, interrupting someone to explain a project or express your opinion.
  5. You can discuss the quality of a product or service.
  6. You can explain the hypotheses and their results in English.
BACK

B1+. Advanced intermediate

⚠️ DIFFICULTY: 🔴🔴🔴🔴 Get to know: 📌 the understanding of the main ideas of complex text on both concrete and abstract topics 📌 how to maintain fluent interactions 📌 how to produce clear, detailed text or speach on a wide range of subjects
  1. At B1+ level, you can communicate competently and comfortably in many professional and personal contexts, and you will find many ways to formulate what you want to explain.
  2. You can be sufficient even in difficult social situations where unfamiliar words come across.
  3. You can engage in conversations with native speakers, ask for clarification when needed, deal with complex situations such as consulting a lawyer, and speak English on the phone for extended periods of time.
  4. You can express your ideas in English and easily overcome unexpected problems.
  5. At B1+ English level, you can grasp detailed information on unfamiliar topics and use the language as a tool to work in other academic and professional fields.
  6. At this level, you can initiate and maintain spontaneous conversations, express and defend your opinions in a discussion, and understand some English idioms created by native speakers.
  7. With your knowledge of English, you can run an office, settle a dispute or defend your opinion against a group.
BACK

B2. Upper-intermediate

⚠️ DIFFICULTY: 🔴🔴🔴🔴🔴 Get to know: 📌 how to analise demanding long texts and recognise implicit meaning. 📌 how to express opinion fluently and spontaneously. 📌 how to use English flexibly and effectively for social, academic and professional purposes. 📌 clear, well-structured, detailed text on complex subjects
  1. You communicate effectively and appropriately even in difficult communicative tasks and situations; like leading a meeting or conference session.
  2. You can meet with business people, give a professional presentation, and have a sales meeting.
  3. You are very fluent and versatile in expressing yourself;
  4. You can easily speak and understand English even in difficult subjects.
  5. There may be minor mistakes in your English speaking and barriers from your native language.
  6. You can communicate effectively in English across a wide range of communities.
  7. You have a good command of the English language.
  8. You can confidently and effectively participate in meetings and discussions, defend your opinions in English in multi-person discussions, give clear and detailed presentations, and use appropriate English language in sensitive social situations.
  9. At this level, you communicate effectively with various audiences on a wide range of familiar and new topics to meet most personal, academic or professional demands, including many which presume experience in public speaking and critical listening.
  10. REQUIRED TO: participate in academic studies at universities, receive acceptance from universities abroad, and work comfortably in a company abroad.
BACK

C1. Advanced

⚠️ DIFFICULTY: 🔴🔴🔴🔴🔴🔴🔴🔴 You will be able to: 📌 understand virtually all types of texts. 📌 summarise information from different oral and written sources, reconstructing arguments and accounts in a coherent presentation. 📌 express yourself spontaneously, very fluently and precisely, differentiating finer shades of meaning even in more complex situations.
  1. At the professional level, you have full command of the language.
  2. You understand and can use virtually all linguistic structures; as well as a range of vocabulary items as broad and deep as that of most educated native speakers.
  3. Communication is fluent, appropriate and well organized—you develop ideas in speech clearly and coherently.
  4. For example, a Frenchman speaks French at C level. If you say you know C-level English, you need to understand and speak as much English as an American or British.
