The last of Mohicans

by James Fenimore Cooper

CHAPTER 3

  With fall approaching, the evenings were getting colder. Two men were talking by a river. One of them had dark skin and his body was painted with different colors. He had a shaved head, apart from a crest down the middle and his name was Chingachgook. He wore buckskin pants and he held a tomahawk and a rifle. The other man was of European origin, but his skin was dark from the sun. He was dressed like a Native American too, and had a rifle-of great length. Chingachgook was telling him stories of his fathers, who were proud warriors before the white men came from the setting sun.
  'l wish there were more of my people. But when I die, my son Uncas, will be the last of the Mohicans,' said Chingachgook.
  'Uncas is here!' said another voice, and a young warrior came and sat with them. The father showed no surprise and they all sat silently for several minutes.
'Did you see any of those thieves from the Maquas tribe?' asked Chingachgook.
  'I have been on their trail, and there are as many as the fingers on both my hands. But they hide like cowards!' replied his son.
  Chingacbgook turned to his friend and said: "Hawkeye, let us eat well tonight and show those Maquas that we are men tomorrow.'
lt was not long before they heard an animal moving in the bushes. Hawkeye raised his rifle, but Uncas whispered to him.
  'If l were you, I wouldn't shoot. The Maquas will hear. Those thieves would want to eat too if you killed the animal.'
  Hawkeye put his rifle down and watched as Uncas threw himself to the ground and moved silently towards his prey. Soon enough, there was an arrow in its side. The wounded animal jumped out of the bushes and Uncas finished the job with his knife.
  'Shhhh! I bear something,' said Chingachgook.
  'Maybe it's wolves following the animal,' suggested Hawkeye.
  'No. The horses of white men are coming. Hawkeye, they are your brothers, speak to them. With the Maquas nearby, this is a dangerous place for them,' said Chingachgook.
