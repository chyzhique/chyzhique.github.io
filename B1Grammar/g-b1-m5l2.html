Reported Speech (Statements)
Special Introductory Verbs

A. Read the following extracts from the dialogue and complete the speech bubbles with the speakers' exact words.

  1. ... he said he would pick me up in five minutes.
    • 🗣️ "I pick up in five minutes".
  2. He said that he had arrived just minutes after I had called him the first time, but I had been nowhere in sight.
    • 🗣️ I arrived just minutes after called the first time, but were nowhere in sight.
  3. ... they kept telling me that I was going to be all right.
    • 🗣️ going to be all right.
  4. ... the doctor who examined me said my knee seemed perfectly fine and I didn't need an operation after all!
    • 🗣️ knee perfectly fine. You need an operation after all.

B. Study the examples above.

📌 We use Reported Speech when we report what someone said without using their exact words.
📌 We usually use the verbs 'say' to introduce reported statements.
📌 We use 'tell' when there is an indirect object (e.g. me, him, etc), but we use 'say' when there is no indirect object.

📌 When we change from Direct to Reported Speech:
- Pronouns and Possessive adjectives change according to the meaning of the sentence.
- Tenses and modal verbs change as in the Grammar Book.
