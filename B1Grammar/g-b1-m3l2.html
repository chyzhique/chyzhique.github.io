MUST - HAVE TO - NEED

A. Look at the following extracts from the dialogue. What do the verbs in bold mean? Choose a, b or c.

- You needn't get a huge sandwich.

- No, we don't have to. It will be fun.



B. Now look at a different version of the extracts. What do the verbs in bold mean?

- You mustn't get a huge sandwich.



- Yes, we have to / need to / must. We have an evening class.



HAD BETTER - WOULD RATHER

Read the following extracts from the dialogue.

- Which of the verbs in bold means would prefer and which means should?
- Which verb form is used after the two verbs?

- We'd better go shopping last ...
- I'd rather buy new clothes than look at old ones.

NOTE

I'd better is the short form of I had better and
I'd rather is the short form of I would rather.
Their negative forms are I'd better not and I'd rather not.
