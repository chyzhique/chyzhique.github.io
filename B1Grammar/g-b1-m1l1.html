B. Read about the uses of the Present Simple and the Present Progressive and think of one more example for each use.

The Present Simple is used:

1. permanent situations,
e.g. Mr Chin lives in China
2. repeated habitual actions,
e.g. ... the park often holds festivals.
3. general truth,
e.g. The Moon goes round the Earth

The Present Progressive is used:

1. actions happening now,
e.g. We are waiting to go down the Colorado River...
2. temporary situations,
e.g. This week we are holding an Indian week.
3. future arrangements,
e.g. Some friends of ours are visiting us next week.
