An informal e·mail to a fnend is similar in content and organisation to an informal letter and, on the whole, the conventions that apply to informal letters apply also to informal e-mails. When writing an e-mail or letter based on prompts:
  • Read the prompts carefully and make sure you include all the points in the notes in your answer
  • Do not copy the notes given. Try to rephrase them, add comments and information, and use expressions to show how you feel.
  • Use linking words to connect your ideas
  • Use standard grammar and spelling conventions. Avoid forms such as wanna, cu I8r etc
  • Use appropriate phrases/expressions to express enthusiasm, give informatton, accept decline invitations, recommend, suggest etc.
