AN E-MAIL BASED ON PROMPTS

A. Linda has received an e-mail from her friend Jennifer.
Read Jennifer's e-mail with the notes Linda has made and answer the questions.

❓ Why is Jennifer writing to Linda?
❓ What does Linda have to do?

B. Now read Linda's reply and underline the sentences which correspond to her notes. Is the wording in Linda's e-mail exactly the same as in her notes?

C. Read the situations 1-5 and the notes made. How would you reply?

WRITING TASK

Imagine that you have received an e-mail from a friend. Read your friend's e-mail and the notes you have made and write a reply using all your notes. Your e-mail should be between 100-120 words.

I just wanted to let you know that I'm taking part in a local music festival this year. I'm a bit nervous though and I'm inviting all my friends to come. of course! You know, having friendly faces around makes me feel more comfortable.

The festival's from Friday the 12th to Sunday the 14th. I'm performing on Saturday, but can't / meet on Saturday? you should come to town on Friday, so we can spend some time together. Apart from musical performances, there are ticket? how much? many fun activities to do at the festival, like watch comedy acts, taste delicious food from around the world, enter competitions and many more.

I really think you should come and be there to cheer for me. You'll have a great time.

Hope you can make it!

When writing an e-mail based on prompts:

  • use appropriate expressions to begin and end your e-mail.
  • read the prompts carefully and make sure you include all the points in the notes in your reply.
  • do not copy the notes given. Try to rephrase them, add comments and information, and use expressions to show how you feel.
  • group related ideas together and divide your e-mail into paragraphs.
  • use appropriate phrases/expressions to invite, accept an invitation, refuse an invitation and give an excuse, make arrangements.
